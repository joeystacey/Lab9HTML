About Me



Introduction

I am a student at BYU in the Information Technology and Cybersecurity major. I work as a Tier 1 Tech for DSO company HQ'd in Denton, TX called Community Dental Partners. When I am not working, in class, or studying, I enjoy playing soccer, pickleball, and spending time outside.

Section 1: Education

My educational background is a bit less than average, which I'll partly explain in the following section "Work Experience". My first 10 years of primary education is fairly normal. In the 11th grade, COVID-19 shut down in person school, forcing us all onto online class and learning. After about 2 months of teachers figuring out their new curriculum and me transitioning to a new style of learning, I ultimately decided that it was in my best interest to drop out of public high school, begin taking online classes through a community college, and start working on my trade of choice. My senior year consisted of working 35-45 hours a week at an IT support desk and taking online classes in order to graduate with my high school degree.

Following an edited version of high school, I entered BYU as a freshman. Unsure of what I wanted to study I first declared as a computer science major. After talking to friends and relatives, I decided that I wanted to pursue healthcare and become a surgeon. That was short-lived. After taking 1 semester of chemistry, I quickly learned that majoring in biology was not right for me. I decided to come back to technology, only this time I began pursuing a degree in Information Systems. That too was short-lived once I learned what it was really about. I now have declared myself as a Cybersecurity major, hoping that I can get a master's with an emphasis in PenTesting/Red-Teaming.

Section 2: Work Experience

I got my first job at 14 as a soccer referee. I did this for about 2 years until I got a job as a lifeguard. I then worked as a lifeguard for 2 years, up until my senior year when I began working at Community Dental Partners at an entry-level position. Here, my responsibilities consisted of answering inbound calls, resolving tickets, fixing hardware/software, and resolving tech "fires". After a year, I decided to leave to come to college. I've since returned to that same company and am currently working as a Tier 1 Tech. As of right now, I am looking for something more focused on cybersecurity and AI automation in order to get more valid work experience for my future career.
